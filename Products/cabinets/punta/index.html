Punta

marble sideboard cabinet

Geometry carved in stone. The Punta transforms Nero Marquina into something closer to architecture — each door panel sculpted into angular facets that fragment and redirect light, shifting from matte to mirror as you move.

The Faceted Form

Light Broken into Planes

The Punta's door panels are not flat — they are carved. Each face is divided into angular, triangular planes that emerge from a central ridge, so the stone reads differently at every angle: matte in one light, mirror-bright in another. No other finish can do this. Only marble.

Brass as Precision

Where the Punta's facets are all movement and shadow, the hand-brushed brass frame is its counterbalance — a clean, exact containing edge that holds the sculptural volume in place. The contrast between the geometric stone and the quiet warmth of the brass is the whole conversation.

Available in four stones

Stone & Metal

The Punta is offered in four marble selections, each transforming the faceted form differently. Nero Marquina's dark ground makes the angular planes emerge like cut obsidian. Lighter stones — Calacatta, Statuario — dissolve the geometry into something quieter and more luminous. Each is paired with hand-brushed brass.

Each door panel begins as a flat marble slab, which is then hand-cut into its angular, triangular planes by our stone carvers. The ridges and recesses are shaped progressively — no two panels are identical, as the natural variation in the marble's density means each responds to the carving differently.

Yes. The Punta is available with polished brass, brushed brass, and patinated bronze finishes. Blackened steel and bespoke lacquer colours are also available on request — particularly effective when paired with lighter marble selections.

The internal cavity measures approximately 170 × 40 × 70 cm with one adjustable shelf. Custom configurations — including pull-out trays, integrated lighting, and divided compartments — are available on request.
